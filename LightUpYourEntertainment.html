Light Up Your Entertainment and Rise Above the Rest

  • Dec 10, 2023
  • 8 min read
  • Project

Do you remember seeing a short movie clip online and instantly wanting to watch the full movie? You go to Google, try your best to describe it, and after 30 minutes of searching, you give up, frustrated, as the dream of watching that movie slips away. Or imagine you're having a perfect movie night with friends, right at the climax scene of your favorite film, endless buffering starts. That feeling of wanting to break the screen? We've all been there.

Well, say no more. Let me introduce Phoenix, the streaming platform designed to solve these problems and more. Phoenix isn’t just another app—it’s your flawless entertainment experience without interruptions. No more frustration over missing out on that must-watch movie. We've got your back, making sure you find exactly what you’re looking for and enjoy your movies, stress-free.

My Role in The Journey of Crafting Pheonix

As the sole designer behind Phoenix, I took full responsibility for both the User Experience(UX) and User Interface(UI) design. This case study was my independent project, where I led every aspect of its design, from research to the final design, relying on my own insights and design instincts.

Main Task:

  • User Insights & Ideation
  • Building the Project Vision
  • Planning and Scope Definition
  • User Interface & Experience

Conclusion

By combining QR code technology with a refined subscription experience, we’re on the way of transforming how Americans receive their water. No more delays, no more confusion—just a simple, reliable service tailored to their needs. With this new approach, Walmart can lead the way in creating a water delivery system that’s fast, flexible, and user-friendly.
